Introduction

Background

Hello! My name is Hamza Ben alla, and I am a fourth-year international student from Morocco. I grew up in the city of Casablanca, whose name translates to “White House.” Although the city is now filled with buildings of many colors, I like to imagine there was once a single white house that inspired its name, this was childhood theory of mine.

As a child, I shared the same passion for video games as most kids my age, yet I was also captivated by the inner workings of electronics, so much so that my relatives would often hide their phones to keep me from experimenting with them, fearful that I might cause damage. In truth, I never truly broke anything, but I might have played with their setting just a few times, which might sometimes resulted in minor mishaps. This fascination with understanding how computers and electronics function ultimately led me to pursue a degree in computer science.

Education

I'm a fourth-year computer science honours student at Acadia University with a focus on research and development in AI. Currently, my honours thesis investigates the early detection of Alzheimer's disease by employing a robust Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model for image classification, integrating MRI and PET scans.
